Friedrich Nietszche - Beyond Good and Evil

Friedrich Nietzsche (1844–1900) was a German philosopher, cultural critic, and writer known for his profound and often provocative ideas about morality, religion, and the nature of existence.
He challenged traditional values and the foundations of Western thought, famously declaring that “God is dead,” a statement reflecting his view that modern society had moved beyond the need for religious belief.
Nietzsche explored concepts such as the “Übermensch” (Overman), the “will to power,” and “eternal recurrence,” encouraging individuals to create their own values and live authentically in a world without inherent meaning.
His work deeply influenced existentialism, psychology, and modern philosophy, leaving a legacy that continues to spark debate and interpretation today.
